Recommendations

Based on our research findings, here are actionable steps communities can take to establish successful garden projects that improve local food security and build stronger neighborhood connections.

Quick Wins

Immediate Actions You Can Take

Start a composting program using kitchen scraps
Organize seed swaps with community members
Partner with local schools for educational programs
Apply for municipal or foundation grants
Connect with master gardener volunteers
Establish relationships with local gardeners

Implementation Roadmap

Community Building

High Priority

Form a Garden Committee

Week 1-2

Establish a diverse group of 5-7 community members to lead the initiative

Community Needs Assessment

Week 3-4

Survey residents to understand food access challenges and gardening interests

Stakeholder Engagement

Week 5-8

Connect with local government, schools, and community organizations for support

Site Planning

High Priority

Site Selection

Week 2-4

Identify suitable locations with 6+ hours of sunlight, water access, and community accessibility

Soil Testing

Week 5-6

Test soil pH, nutrients, and contaminants to determine needed amendments

Design Layout

Week 7-8

Create plot assignments, pathways, composting areas, and tool storage plans

Garden Setup

Medium Priority

Infrastructure Development

Week 9-12

Install raised beds, irrigation systems, fencing, and tool storage

Soil Preparation

Week 13-14

Add compost, organic matter, and necessary amendments based on soil test results

Plant Selection

Week 15-16

Choose climate-appropriate, culturally diverse crops with staggered planting schedules
