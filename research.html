Research Findings

Our comprehensive research explores the impact of community gardens on food security, economic development, and environmental sustainability. Below are key findings from peer-reviewed studies and expert analyses.

Key Research Studies

Urban Agriculture and Food Security

Academic Study Source: Journal of Urban Agriculture

Studies show that community gardens can increase local food access by up to 40% in underserved areas while providing nutritional education and community building opportunities.

Key Findings:

  • 40% increase in fresh produce access
  • Reduced food transportation costs
  • Enhanced community social cohesion
  • Improved nutritional knowledge among participants

Community Gardens Economic Impact

Economic Analysis Source: Economic Development Research Institute

Economic analysis reveals that every R17.65 invested in community gardens generates R105.9 in community economic benefits through reduced healthcare costs, increased property values, and local food production.

Key Findings:

  • 6:1 return on investment
  • Increased property values by 9.4%
  • Reduced healthcare costs
  • Job creation in local food systems

Sustainable Gardening Practices

Environmental Study Source: Sustainable Agriculture Research Foundation

Research demonstrates that organic community gardening methods can produce 30% more vegetables per square foot compared to traditional methods while improving soil health and biodiversity.

Key Findings:

  • 30% higher yield per square foot
  • Improved soil microbiome diversity
  • Reduced water usage through mulching
  • Natural pest management effectiveness
